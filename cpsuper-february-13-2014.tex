Our potential is 
\beq
V=\frac{N}{4\pi}\left\{-iD\ln{\frac{2|\sigma|}{\Lambda^2}}
+iD\,\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^k}{k(k+1)} \left(\frac{iD}{2|\sigma|^2}\right)^k
\right\}
\eeq
The first term comes from Witten's superpotential. The first term of the series (with k=1)
is taken into account by kinetic term. Thus we can write our effective action as
\beqn
S &=& 
\frac{N}{4\pi}\int d^4 x\left\{\int d^4\theta \frac12\left|\ln{\Sigma}\right|^2
\right.
\nonumber\\
&&
-i\int d^2\theta\left[\sqrt{2}\Sigma \ln{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}-\sqrt{2}\Sigma
\right.
\nonumber\\
&&
\left.\left.
-\frac12\,\sqrt{2}\Sigma\,\sum_{k=2}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^k}{k(k+1)(k-1)} 
\left(\frac{S}{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}\right)^k
\right]\right\} +c.c
\eeqn
Is this expression correct? Here I use your definition of $S$.

The series in the third line can be written as a $D$-term. We get
\beqn
S &=& 
\frac{N}{4\pi}\int d^4 x\left\{\int d^4\theta \frac12\left|\ln{\Sigma}\right|^2
\right.
\nonumber\\
&&
-i\int d^2\theta\left[\sqrt{2}\Sigma \ln{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}-\sqrt{2}\Sigma\right]
\nonumber\\
&&
\left.
-\int d^4\theta\frac{1}{2}\,\ln{\bar{\Sigma}}\,\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{(k+1)}}{k(k+1)(k+2)} 
\left(\frac{S}{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}\right)^k
 +c.c. \right\}
\eeqn
Here we see explicitly that the only $F$-term is the Witten's superpotential while all 
other terms are $D$-terms.


The last term can be rewritten as 
\beqn
&&
S = 
\frac{N}{4\pi}\int d^4 x\left\{\int d^4\theta \frac12\left|\ln{\Sigma}\right|^2
-i\int d^2\theta\left[\sqrt{2}\Sigma \ln{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}-\sqrt{2}\Sigma\right]
\right.
\nonumber\\
&&
-\int d^4\theta\frac{1}{2}\,\ln{\bar{\Sigma}}\,\left[\frac12\left(1+\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}{S}\right)
\right)^2\,\ln{\left(1+\left(\frac{S}{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}\right)\right)} -\frac34 
-\frac12\frac{\sqrt{2}\Sigma}{S}
\right] 
\nonumber\\
&&
\left.
+c.c. \right\}
\eeqn
